ECOFEET

Who we are?

We've been developing high-tech new technology to apply in the shoe industry for many years. We are a fully integrated company that produces for more than 100 top fashion brands.

No brand, no company, no institution can argue against the fact that faux fur should replace all animal furs.

 

The fashion trends have forced us to invest in new machineries to respond to this big new wave of shoe original design that is becoming a key item in all brands. Even the most traditional luxury brands are including a lot of knitted sneakers into their main collections.

 

Long an advocate for innovation, and committed to environmental protection and corporate social responsibility, ECOFEET aims to make a difference in the textile and apparel industry and wants to add its positive contribution to the way fashion is created.

The Race To Create Knitted Shoes That Cut The Wastefulness Of Our Footwear

 

Making a sneaker usually involves quite a bit of work and time. The process also creates waste, since every time a piece of a shoe is cut out, extra pieces are left behind. But if the shoe is knitted instead of sewn, suddenly there’s almost no wasted material–and all of the work can be done by a machine.
January 1, 2019

 

The unprecedented boom of knitted shoes since in 2012, Nike and Adidas released their first styles

 

Nike was first–it actually sued Adidas for patent infringement when the German company came out with a similar shoe months later. But Adidas says that the timing was just an example of the same idea bubbling to the surface in different parts of the market because both manufacturers were searching for innovative and more sustainable techniques.

Pharrell's adidas NMD Hu Trail "Know/Soul" knitted sneaks are featuring a Neon comeback

 

Pharrell‘s fondness for color is quite apparent among his many adidas collaborations with the latest atop the NMD Hu Trail — dubbed fittingly as the “Know/Soul” — taking on blinding neon greens throughout. A sort of return to form, the pair evokes strong connection to the debuting originals, though with modifications by way of trail- appropriate treads.

Supreme for a new take on the Air Jordan XIV

 

Following their first collaboration in 2015, Jordan Brand and Supreme New York come together again. Using the sports-car-inspired silhouette as its base, Supreme brings a bold new look to the Air Jordan XIV's upper on a white, black and University Red colourway with stud detailing and subtle Supreme branding throughout.

Adidas Commits To Sustainability With 100% Recyclable Shoe
